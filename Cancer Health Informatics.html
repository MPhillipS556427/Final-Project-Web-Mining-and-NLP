Sentiment Analysis Explanation:¶

  • Sentiment polarity suggests a slightly positive sentiment. It means that the overall tone of the news article is leaning towards being positive.

  • Sentiment subjectivity indicates moderate subjectivity, this means that while the article contains some objective information, it also includes a fair amount of subjective or opinion-based content.

  • The positive sentiment polarity suggests that the article's content is likely discussing positive developments or advancements in cancer treatment. The subjectivity score indicates that the article may not only provide factual information about the trial results but could also have some subjective elements, such as opinions from medical experts, patients, or researchers regarding the significance of these findings.

from sklearn.feature_extraction.text import TfidfVectorizer
from sklearn.cluster import KMeans
import numpy as np


article_texts = [
    "Scientists have convened in the United States city of Chicago for a meeting...",
    "At the annual event, which wraps up on Tuesday, doctors and researchers...",
    "Developed by the pharmaceutical group AstraZeneca, the daily pill targets...",
    "Iris Pauporte, head of research at France’s League Against Cancer, told AFP...",
    "Another treatment, called vorasidenib, was found to significantly prolong...",
    "Preliminary trial results also released in Chicago indicated the drug...",
    "A trial also presented at ASCO showed that taking the antibody treatment...",
    "Preliminary studies announced at the ASCO meeting involved vaccines...",
]


tfidf_vectorizer = TfidfVectorizer(stop_words='english')
tfidf_matrix = tfidf_vectorizer.fit_transform(article_texts)


num_clusters = 3
kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=num_clusters, random_state=0)
kmeans.fit(tfidf_matrix)


cluster_labels = kmeans.labels_


article_clusters = {}
for i, label in enumerate(cluster_labels):
    if label not in article_clusters:
        article_clusters[label] = []
    article_clusters[label].append(article_texts[i])


for cluster_id, articles in article_clusters.items():
    print(f"Cluster {cluster_id} articles:")
    for article in articles:
        print(article)
    print("=" * 50)
